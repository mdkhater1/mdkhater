A2+ Writing Practice

Write a clear paragraph with topic sentence, details, and conclusion

How to Write a Good Paragraph (A2+)

A strong paragraph has three main parts:

  1. Topic sentence – the first sentence, gives the main idea.
  2. Supporting sentences – 3–5 sentences with reasons, details, or examples.
  3. Concluding sentence – the last sentence, sums up or gives your opinion.

Steps for A2+ Students

  1. Read the topic and decide your main idea.
  2. Write one clear topic sentence.
  3. Add 3–5 supporting sentences with simple linking words: and, but, because, also, for example.
  4. Finish with a short concluding sentence.
  5. Check grammar, spelling, and punctuation.

Example structure:

  • Sentence 1 – Topic sentence (main idea).
  • Sentences 2–5 – Details, reasons, examples.
  • Sentence 6 – Concluding sentence.

Topic 1: My Favorite Room

Task: Write a paragraph (80–100 words) about your favorite room at home.

Include: where it is, what is inside, what you do there, and why you like it.

Plan Your Paragraph

  • Topic sentence: Say which room is your favorite.
  • Supporting sentences: Describe furniture, activities, people, and feelings.
  • Concluding sentence: Say why it is special for you.

Model Paragraph (A2+)

My favorite room in my home is the living room. It is a bright room with a big, soft sofa and a small coffee table. I like to sit there in the evening and watch TV or read a book. On weekends, I spend time with my family in the living room, and we talk, laugh, and play games together. The walls are light and there are some family photos that make me feel happy. It is my favorite room because I can relax there and enjoy time with the people I love.

Topic 2: Ways to Help Our Environment

Task: Write a paragraph (80–100 words) about how people can help the environment.

Include: recycling, saving energy, reducing waste, and protecting nature.

Plan Your Paragraph

  • Topic sentence: Say that we can help the environment in different ways.
  • Supporting sentences: Give 3–4 clear ideas with examples.
  • Concluding sentence: Explain why these actions are important.

Useful Linking Words

  • Add ideas: also, moreover, another way
  • Give examples: for example, for instance
  • Result: so, because of this

Model Paragraph (A2+)

We can help our environment in many simple ways in our daily lives. First, we should recycle paper, plastic, and glass instead of throwing them in the bin. We can also save energy by turning off lights and electronic devices when we do not need them. Another way to help is to use less plastic, for example by taking a reusable bag to the supermarket. We can plant trees and keep our streets clean. If everyone makes small changes, the planet will be cleaner and safer for future generations.

Topic 3: Life in the Future

Task: Write a paragraph (80–100 words) about life in the future.

Include: homes, technology, travel, work or study, and your opinion.

Plan Your Paragraph

  • Topic sentence: Say that life in the future will be different.
  • Supporting sentences: Talk about technology, travel, jobs, or school.
  • Concluding sentence: Say if you think the future will be good or bad, and why.

Useful Future Phrases

  • will for predictions: people will travel, homes will be smarter.
  • might for possibilities: students might learn online.

Model Paragraph (A2+)

Life in the future will be very different from life today. Technology will make our homes smarter, and people will control lights and machines with their phones or even their voices. Many people will work from home and use computers to meet their colleagues online. Travel will probably be faster, and some tourists might visit space. Students will use more digital tools and learn from teachers in different countries. Some things may be difficult, but I think life in the future will be exciting and full of new opportunities for many people.

Checklist for A2+ Paragraphs

  • Do I have 80–100 words?
  • Do I start with a clear topic sentence?
  • Do I use 3–5 supporting sentences with details and examples?
  • Do I finish with a short concluding sentence?
  • Did I check spelling, punctuation, and basic grammar?
