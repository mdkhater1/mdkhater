Properties of Matter

Ali Mustafa - Grade 5D

What is matter?

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. Scientists describe matter by observing or measuring its physical properties.

⚖️

Has mass

You can measure how much matter is in an object.

📦

Takes up space

Everything made of matter has volume.

👀

Can be observed

Some properties can be seen right away.

📏

Can be measured

Others need tools like rulers, scales, or cups.

States of matter

🧊

Solid

A solid has its own shape and its own volume because its particles are packed tightly.


💧

Liquid

A liquid keeps its volume but changes shape to fit its container because particles can move past each other.


☁️

Gas

A gas has no fixed shape or fixed volume because particles spread out and move freely.


Observable properties

🎨

Color

Red, blue, clear, or shiny.

🔷

Shape

Round, square, long, or flat.

✋

Texture

Smooth, rough, soft, or bumpy.

👃

Odor

Some materials have a smell.

🧲

Magnetism

Some materials are attracted to magnets.

💡

Transparency

Objects can be clear, cloudy, or opaque.

Measurable properties

Mass and volume

  • Mass tells how much matter is in an object.
  • Volume tells how much space an object takes up.
  • Tools include a balance, ruler, or measuring cup.

Other useful tests

  • Solubility shows whether something dissolves in water.
  • Conductivity shows whether heat or electricity can move through it.
  • Density helps explain why some objects sink and others float.

How matter can change

❄️

Freezing

Liquid water freezes into solid ice at 0°C.

🔥

Melting

Solid ice melts into liquid water at 0°C.

💨

Boiling

Liquid water boils into gas at 100°C.

These are physical changes because the substance is still water. Only the state changes, not the kind of matter.

Remember

Matter has mass and takes up space. We can identify matter by looking at properties such as state, color, texture, mass, volume, density, solubility, and conductivity.
