1. Vocabulary – Art, Photography & Selfies

1.1 Study the words (word – definition – example)

Art & design professions

designer
Someone who plans and creates the look of things such as posters, clothes or websites.
Max is a graphic designer who created a modern layout for the festival posters.
printmaker
An artist who makes many copies of the same image, often using a press or special machine.
The printmaker showed us how to make a print of our drawings for the school exhibition.
sculptor
An artist who creates figures and forms from materials like clay, stone or wood.
The sculptor made an original sculpture for the park using only recycled wood.
potter
Someone who makes pots and other objects from clay on a potter’s wheel.
The potter threw a pot quickly and turned it into a beautiful clay bowl.
photographer
A person who takes photos, especially as a job.
David Slater is a wildlife photographer who travelled to Indonesia to take pictures of macaques.
🎨
ART JOBS
designer · printmaker · sculptor · potter · photographer

Artworks & objects

sculpture
A work of art made by shaping stone, wood, metal or clay into a form.
We saw a huge sculpture made of wood in the garden during the art festival.
pottery
Objects made from clay and baked in a kiln, such as bowls or vases.
In the workshop, we practised pottery and created colourful clay pots.
print
A copy of a picture or design produced by pressing ink onto paper or fabric.
The artist decided to make a print of her digital drawing to give copies to her students.
self-portrait
A picture that an artist makes of themselves.
The first ever self-portrait was painted by an Italian artist in the sixteenth century.
installation art
A large artwork that fills a space and often uses many objects or figures.
Field for the British Isles is an installation with thousands of small clay figures.
🗿
ARTWORKS
sculpture · pottery · print · self‑portrait · installation

Photography tools & actions

tripod
A stand with three legs used to hold a camera still.
David set up his camera on a tripod to keep it perfectly still during the photo shoot.
lens
The glass part of a camera that focuses light to make a clear image.
The photographer cleaned the lens before the session so the photos would be in focus.
press the shutter
To push the button on a camera to take a photo.
Naruto the macaque pressed the shutter and took a famous monkey selfie.
selfie-stick
A stick that holds your phone so you can take a selfie from further away.
You can’t use a selfie-stick in some museums and theme parks for safety reasons.
photo shoot
A session when a photographer takes many photos, usually for a project.
Max organised a professional photo shoot for a famous fashion magazine.
📷
CAMERA KIT
tripod · lens · press the shutter · selfie‑stick · photo shoot

Editing & appearance

retouch / edit
To change a photo, for example by correcting colours or removing mistakes.
Max used software to retouch the photos and make the colours more vibrant.
add filters
To apply special effects to a photo to change its style or colours.
Many young people add filters to their selfies before they share them online.
photogenic
Looking attractive in photos.
Because Naruto is so photogenic, his selfie looks like a professional portrait.
in / out of focus
In focus = clear and sharp; out of focus = not clear or blurry.
The first photo was out of focus, so we took another one with the subject in focus.
go viral
To become very popular on the internet very quickly.
The monkey selfie went viral and was shared by millions of people.
✨
EDITING
retouch · add filters · photogenic · focus · go viral

3. Reading Comprehension – Exam Style

3.1 Reading 1 – The Famous Monkey Selfie

The Story of the Famous Monkey Selfie

In 2011, a British wildlife photographer named David Slater travelled to Indonesia for a professional photo shoot. He wanted to capture the perfect images of crested macaques, which are rare monkeys known locally as yaki. To get the best results, David set up his camera on a professional tripod and checked the lens to make sure the light was perfect.

While David was busy, a curious macaque called Naruto approached the equipment. Naruto looked at his own reflection in the glass and began to play. Suddenly, Naruto decided to press the shutter himself. The result was an amazing self-portrait of a smiling monkey. Because Naruto was naturally photogenic, the picture looked professional and was perfectly in focus.

David decided to share the photo online, and it did not take long for the image to go viral. Millions of people saw the monkey selfie on social media. However, a big problem started when people asked who owned the photo. A group called PETA sued the photographer, arguing that Naruto should own the copyright. Eventually, a settlement was reached. David remained the owner, but he agreed to donate twenty-five per cent of his future earnings to protect the macaques’ habitat.

A. Are the sentences true (T) or false (F)?

1. David Slater went to Indonesia to take photos of monkeys.

2. The monkey used a selfie-stick to take the famous photo.

3. The photo became very popular and went viral online.

4. David had to give all of his money to wildlife charities.

5. The yaki is a type of rare monkey found in Indonesia.

6. The monkey’s name in the story is David.

B. Answer the questions in complete sentences.

  1. Why did David use a tripod during his photo shoot?
  2. What did David agree to do with 25% of his earnings?
Compare your answers with the hints and underline key words in the text.

3.2 Reading 2 – The Career of a Graphic Designer

The Career of a Graphic Designer

In the modern world, a designer does more than just draw. Today, digital graphic design is used in everything from book covers to social media advertisements. Max is a professional designer who started his career by studying traditional painting and pottery, but he soon discovered a passion for technology.

Last year, Max was hired for a major project: a professional photo shoot for a fashion magazine. He did not just take the photos; he used his skills to retouch the images, making the colours more vibrant and ensuring every detail was in focus. He also added creative filters to give the photos an abstract look.

When Max shared his work online, it did not take long for the images to go viral. Millions of people praised his original style. However, Max believes that even with modern technology, the most important part of art is the idea behind it. “A computer is just a tool,” he says. “Whether you are a sculptor working with clay or a designer using a screen, you must first have a brilliant vision.”

A. Are the sentences true (T) or false (F)?

1. Max started his artistic career by working only with digital computers.

2. During the project, Max edited the photos to make them look abstract.

3. Max believes that the tools are more important than the artist’s idea.

4. The photos became very popular after they were shared on the internet.

5. Max has experience in traditional arts like pottery.

6. The text suggests that graphic design is only used for magazines.

B. Answer the questions in complete sentences.

  1. What did Max do to the photos to ensure they were of high quality?
  2. In Max’s opinion, what is the role of a computer in the world of art?
Use the hints to self-correct your answers.
