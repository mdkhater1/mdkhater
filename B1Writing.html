1. What is an opinion essay?

üí°

Opinion essay = a piece of writing where you say what you think about a topic and explain why.

  • You give your opinion clearly.
  • You support it with reasons and examples.
  • You usually use a formal style (no slang, careful grammar).

2. Basic structure (4‚Äì5 paragraphs)

A typical opinion essay has the following parts:

  • Paragraph 1 ‚Äì Introduction
  • Paragraph 2 ‚Äì Body 1 (Reason 1)
  • Paragraph 3 ‚Äì Body 2 (Reason 2)
  • Paragraph 4 ‚Äì Conclusion

You can think: Intro ‚Üí Reasons ‚Üí Conclusion.

3. Parts of an opinion essay

START

Introduction

Paragraph 1
  • Introduce the topic (1‚Äì2 sentences).
  • Give some background information.
  • Write a clear thesis statement = your opinion.
Example:

‚ÄúToday, many animal species are in danger of extinction. In my opinion, it is essential to protect endangered animals like the yaki macaques because they are a vital part of our planet.‚Äù

REASON 1

Body Paragraph 1

Paragraph 2
  • Topic sentence = your first main reason.
  • Explain the reason in more detail.
  • Give an example or evidence.
  • Use linking words: Firstly, For example, In addition.
Example topic sentence:

‚ÄúFirstly, endangered animals help keep the balance of nature, so losing them can damage entire ecosystems.‚Äù

REASON 2

Body Paragraph 2

Paragraph 3
  • Topic sentence = your second main reason.
  • Add explanation and details.
  • Give another example or illustration.
  • Use linking words: Secondly, Moreover, However.
Example topic sentence:

‚ÄúSecondly, we have a moral responsibility to protect animals, especially when humans are the main cause of their problems.‚Äù

END

Conclusion

Paragraph 4
  • Summarise your main reasons (no new ideas).
  • Restate your opinion using different words.
  • Finish with a strong final sentence.
Example:

‚ÄúIn conclusion, protecting endangered animals like the yaki macaques is essential for the environment and for future generations. For these reasons, I believe we must act now before it is too late.‚Äù

6. Topic samples and practice

Topic 1 ‚Äì Endangered animals (yaki macaques)

üêí

Question: ‚ÄúIn your opinion, is it important to protect endangered animals like the ‚Äòyaki‚Äô macaques? How can we help them?‚Äù

Sample body paragraph (around 80‚Äì100 words):

When one species disappears, many other plants and animals can be affected. For example, macaques help spread seeds in the forest, which allows new trees to grow and keeps the environment healthy. If we lose them, the balance of nature may be damaged, and future generations will never see these unique animals. For this reason, I believe we must take action now to protect them.

  • A topic sentence about how humans can help endangered animals.
  • At least two ideas (laws, charities, education, etc.).
  • One example of something students can do.

Question: ‚ÄúDo you agree with Max that a computer is ‚Äòjust a tool‚Äô for a real artist? Why or why not?‚Äù

For instance, two artists using the same software will still produce very different pictures, because their ideas and styles are not the same. Practice ‚Äì Support or contrast:

Write another body paragraph for this topic. Choose one option:

  • Option A: Explain how computers make art better or easier (editing, sharing online, special effects).
  • Option B: Explain how computers can sometimes be a problem for art (copying, no original style, too much focus on technology).

Use linking words like Secondly, Moreover, However, On the other hand.
